What if the world was flat?

Have you ever wondered what it would feel like to walk on an Earth which was flat, if the laws of gravity still obeyed Newton's theory? In this report, we will analyse the curvature of a gravitating body, as perceived by an infinitely small observer, with an infinitely-small range of vision.

The general gravitational field strength

In this report, we only consider the curvature perceived, and not the strength of gravity, as the latter is trivially defined by the (not-so-trivial to solve) Gauss' law for gravitation:

\[ \vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{g} = 4 \pi G \rho \]
